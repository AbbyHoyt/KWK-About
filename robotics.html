Robotics

In high school I competed in the VEX Robotics Competition on team 99621A Lemon Pi, an all-girls team, with my twin sister and three of our friends. My roles on the team consisted of designing, building, programming, and keeping an engineering notebook. During my last season I won three awards (including one at the state championship!) and co-authored a 2,200+ page engineering notebook. Although I no longer compete in the VEX Robotics Competition I regularly volunteer as a referee at local competitions and help out at a robotics summer camp to share my love for robotics with my community. Now, I do software and marketing for TrickFire Robotics, the University of Washington Bothell’s University Rover Challenge team!


VEX Robotics Competition Awards:

  • Innovate Award | Cavelero Cup 2024
  • Design Award | Lake Stevens League 2024
  • Judges Award | Washington State Championship 2024




TrickFire Robotics

After enjoying robotics classes and competitions in high school, I was eager to find an outlet for robotics in college and excitedly joined TrickFire Robotics, the University of Washington Bothell’s University Rover Challenge team! The University Rover Challenge asks teams to design and build a rover capable of early Mars exploration. I am a member of TrickFire’s software and marketing subteams. More specifically, I am a member of the Mission Control subteam which is responsible for the communication between the rover and its operators. I enjoy coding, making posters and slideshows, doing outreach events at middle schools and high schools with the team, and helping out wherever else I can! TrickFire has taught me countless new technical skills and left with me lifelong friends and memories.
