Coding

I love to code. I've completed multiple Girls Who Code summer programs where I've learned HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Python as well as gained skills in web development, cybersecurity, cryptography, data science, and AI. In high school, I was a member of my school’s Girls Who Code club chapter for three years and was co-president of the chapter with my sister for two years. Moreover, my sister and I won Girls Who Code's 2023 Spring Challenge, Humanize AI, with our website on the ethics of facial recognition technology and AI. Outside of Girls Who Code, I have taken classes and participated in programs that taught me how to make iOS apps in Swift and SwiftUI using XCode, games in C# using Unity, and way more. I also coded a competition robot using VEXCode in high school and now work on software for my college’s University Rover Challenge team. Besides deepening my own coding skills, I love to teach others how to code and have volunteered to teach middle schoolers how to code robots in Python at my high school's robotics summer camp for multiple years now.

Featured Projects





Bee a Hero: Save the Hive, Save the World!

In April 2025 I participated in UWB Hacks: Save the World!, my first ever hackathon. Bee a Hero: Save the Hive, Save the World! was my team’s submission for the entertainment track and consisted of an informational website and embedded game. We were inspired by the sharp decline in bee populations and hoped to raise awareness and inspire our community to take action to save these pollinators.


Check Out the Project: Bee a Hero: Save the Hive, Save the World! on DevPost






Adan for President

Adan for President is a student government campaign website I made for a friend! Besides serving as a way to effectively communicate campaign goals, the website consists of a countdown timer tracking when voting opened and closed as well as other interactive features.


Check Out the Project: adanpres2025.netlify.app






Shorecrest VEX Robotics Club 99621 Website

This is a multi-page, informational website for Shorecrest High School’s VEX Robotics Competition teams. The website details different aspects of the VEX Robotics Competition, team history and achievements, information about the annual competition and summer camp the team hosts, and ways to donate or volunteer your time to the program.


Check Out the Project: shorelineroboticssociety.org/sc-vex






Facing Bias: The Ethics of Facial Recognition Through AI

Facing Bias: The Ethics of Facial Recognition Through AI is a winning submission for Girls Who Code's 2023 Humanize AI Challenge. In making the website, my sister and I set out to explore the ethics of facial recognition technology and AI, since Apple’s Face ID doesn’t work on twins. After lots of research and interviewing our community about their unique experiences with AI, we compiled the information into an informative, accessible, and eye-catching website.


Check Out the Project: Facing Bias: The Ethics of Facial Recognition Through AI on Replit






Coral Comeback

Coral Comeback is an iOS App I made with two others as a part of Kode With Klossy’s Mobile App Development Program. The app raises awareness about coral reef decline and consists of informational pages, a personalized message generator, quiz, and countdown timer to 2050 (when scientists predict 75% to 100% of coral reefs will be threatened).
